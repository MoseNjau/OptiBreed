About Us

OptiBreed is a student-led initiative from JKUAT, facilitated by JHUB. Since 2024, we have provided innovative IoT-based solutions to monitor and preserve breeding materials across various species.


We support:

  • Smallholder Farmers: Collaborating with local farmers and community seed banks.
  • Agricultural Cooperatives: Assisting cooperative farming groups to enhance storage practices.
  • Government Agricultural Agencies: We collaborate with governmental agricultural bodies to champion sustainable agricultural practices.
  • Research Institutions: Supporting universities like JKUAT in agricultural research and technology dissemination.
  • NGOs: Working with organizations to develop sustainable breeding techniques.

Problem Statement

Inconsistent Monitoring

Manual methods are irregular and error-prone, leading to unreliable data and unnoticed fluctuations in storage conditions.

Lack of Real-Time Data

Without real-time data, stakeholders can't make timely decisions, leading to compromised quality of breeding materials.

Resource Optimization

Traditional methods inefficiently use resources, resulting in higher costs and environmental impact. Automated systems are needed for consistent optimization.

Solution Overview

OptiBreed uses IoT technology for automated, real-time monitoring and management of storage conditions, providing alerts and remote access for optimal environments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Breeding materials include embryos, plant tissue cultures, seed banks, and microbial cultures.

Our system uses IoT sensors to collect real-time data on environmental conditions and provides insights through data analytics and visualization.
